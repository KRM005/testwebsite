Rules and Regulations

Eligibility

  1. The hackathon is open to individuals who are part of VIT, Vellore.
  2. Teams can consist of up to 5 members.
  3. Each participant must register individually on VTop and agree to these rules.

Team Formation

  1. Teams can be formed before the event or during the event's designated team formation period.
  2. Team size must not exceed the specified limit of members.
  3. Participants can only be part of one team.

Project Guidelines

  1. Projects must be started from scratch during the hackathon's official start time.
  2. Use of existing codebases and projects is allowed only if they are publicly available and disclosed at the beginning of the event
  3. All code, assets, and materials must be the original work of the team, except for third-party resources, which must be properly credited.
  4. Plagiarism of code or projects will result in immediate disqualification

Code of Conduct

  1. All participants must adhere to the hackathon's code of conduct, promoting an inclusive and respectful environment.
  2. Harassment or discrimination of any kind will not be tolerated. Violation of this rule will result in immediate removal from the event.
  3. Respect the facilities, equipment, and other participants' workspaces.

Submission Guidelines

  1. Each team must submit their project by the designated deadline.
  2. Submissions should include a brief description of the project, including its purpose, features, and technologies used.
  3. A working demo or prototype should be provided along with the submission.
  4. Late submissions will not be accepted.

Judging Criteria

  1. Projects will be judged based on creativity, technical difficulty, execution, and impact.
  2. Judges' decisions are final and cannot be appealed.

Prizes

  1. Details of prizes will be announced at the beginning of the event.
  2. Prizes are non-transferable and cannot be exchanged.

Intellectual Property

  1. Participants retain ownership of their projects.
  2. By participating, teams grant the hackathon organizers the right to use their project names, descriptions, and visuals for promotional purposes.

Equipment and Tools

  1. Participants are responsible for bringing their own laptops, charger, water bottles, snacks.
  2. Organizers may provide specific software or resources, which will be communicated prior to the event.

Safety and Security

  1. Participants are responsible for the security of their own belongings.
  2. Do not tamper with or attempt to access systems or data that are not your own.

Disqualification

  1. Teams may be disqualified for violating any of the rules or regulations.
  2. Disqualified teams will not be eligible for prizes or recognition.

Changes to Rules

  1. Hackathon organizers reserve the right to make changes to these rules if necessary.
  2. Participants will be informed of any changes.

How to Register

  1. Each participant need to register directly on Vtop first;
    • Go to Event registration
    • Search for HACK-A-TRON
    • Register for it[there is no registration fees]
  2. After signing up on VTOP, you can choose to register either as a team or as an individual
    • Register as Team
    • Register as Individual
  3. Hackathon will start on 18-03-24 at 8 pm and will end at 20-03-24 at 7 am.
